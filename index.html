The Story of Nachiketa

Nachiketa, a young seeker, approached Yama, the Lord of Death, to seek the ultimate truth...

Read more

Nachiketa's father, a sage, performed a ritual where he had to donate his possessions. He offered weak, old cows, angering his son. Nachiketa asked, "Father, to whom will you give me?" His father, irritated, replied, "To Yama, the God of Death!" Nachiketa took it seriously and went to Yama's abode. Yama was absent, so he waited for three days. Pleased by his patience, Yama granted him three boons. Nachiketa asked for his father’s peace, knowledge of fire sacrifice, and the secret of life after death. Yama tested him but finally revealed the eternal truth: **"The soul is immortal and beyond birth and death."**

The Wisdom of King Janaka

A sage asked King Janaka how he remained detached despite being a ruler...

Read more

One day, a sage came to test King Janaka’s detachment. He whispered, “Your palace is on fire!” King Janaka calmly replied, **“The palace may burn, but my inner self remains untouched.”** The sage was amazed. This proved that true wisdom lies in realizing that material possessions are temporary, while the soul remains free.
